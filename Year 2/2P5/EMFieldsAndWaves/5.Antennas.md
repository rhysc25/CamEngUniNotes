One of the simplest antenna designs for broadcasting is the half-dipole or grand-plane antenna
Consists of a conductor of height $\lambda/4$ sticking out of a ground plane
When an AC signal is applied to the antenna, the method of images means there is an effective opposite charge being produced under the ground plane
The wave will be preferentially transmitted in the plane of the ground

Short Dipole Antenna -
Very similar, just with wire length much less than the wavelength of the electromagnetic wave we will be transmitting. $I=I_0\cos(\omega t)$

Using the retarded vector magnetic potential equation and setting the antenna at the origin of the coordinate system so $r_0 = 0$, we get an expression for A, the magnetic potential. We then do $\textbf{B}=\nabla \times \textbf{A}$

From this we get that $B_{\phi}(\rho,\phi,z,t)=\frac{j\beta \rho \mu_0 \mu_r l I_0}{4\pi (\rho^2 + z^2)}exp(j[\omega t -\beta (\rho^2+z^2)^{1/2}])$
It has no component in $\rho$ or $z$ directions. The magnetic flux density just circles around the antenna in the azimuthal direction only.

Taking the curl and integrating gives us an expression for E and we find it has only a $\theta$ component. So the B and E fields are like lines of latitude and longitude respectively
We can find our Poynting vector of the resulting transmitted wave easily by $\frac{|E||H|}{2}$

Both fields decay as $1/r$ with distance away from antenna
The greatest power density is in a plane perpendicular to the line of the antenna
The power per unit area decays as $1/r^2$ with distance from the antenna

To get higher power density: increase input current, increase length of antenna (and subsequently increase input voltage), decrease the wavelength

Figures of merit - 
Antenna gain = maximum power density / isotropic power density
The isotropic power density is just $p/(4\pi r^2)$

Radiation resistance -
The resistance $R_a$ that would have to be put in the place of the transmitting antenna that would dissipate as much power as the antenna radiates $R_a=P/I^2_{rms}$
Have to impedance match

Effective area - 
The area of the electromagnetic wave that would give the power $P_{abs}$ absorbed by the antenna.
$A_{eff}=\frac{P_{abs}}{|\bar{N}|}$. The power being absorbed / the actual power per unit area of the wave

The principle of reciprocity states that the properties of a receiving antenna are the same as if it was a transmitter
$I_B / V_A = I_A / V_B$
