Firm - an organisation consisting of 1 or more individuals working as a decision making unit to produce goods or services. E.g. public or private limited company
Market - an institution for resource allocation

Neoclassical Theory of a Firm:
$Q=K^{0.5}L^{0.5}$ where Q is the constant output and capital K and labour L are the inputs
The optimal depends on the relative price of K and L

**Transaction Cost View of a Firm**
Transaction cost: Search and information, bargaining and decision, policing and enforcement
Firm characterised by incomplete contracts, they economise on transaction cost
The reason why all production is not done at one firm: The transactions are spatially dispersed and dissimilar
**Property Rights View of a Firm**
Ownership of many parts of a good or service matters because it carries residual control, and it's better that one entity has control over it's direction
**Marxist View of a Firm**
Firms exist to control the value of production rather than to improve efficiency
**Managerial View of a Firm**
Managers maximise their own welfare over the good of the company
**Behavioural Theory**
Firms engage in gift exchange with their employees to boost moral and productivity
**Capability View of a Firm**
The firm only exists as it has knowledge of what to do, and is correctly placed to do that thing
**Evolutionary Theory of a Firm**
Competition is a process, like natural selection. Innovation drives structural change.
