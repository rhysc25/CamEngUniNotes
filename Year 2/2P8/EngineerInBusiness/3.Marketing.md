Drucker - business only has two main functions - marketing and innovation
Marketing is about making sure what we innovate meets the needs of the customers

Three levels of marketing:
1) Culture - marketing philosophies
2) Strategy - right customers and right value propositions
3) Tactics - value creation, communication, delivery and capture (product, promotion, place and price)

Price extracts value from customers, the others communication value to customers

Marketing Strategy:
* Segmentation - divide market into distinct groups of customers
* Targeting - select customer group to focus on
* Positioning - differentiating oneself from customers

Marketing culture: Outside in or inside out.
Outside in - external emphasis - understanding customer so well that the product sells itself
Inside out - internal emphasis - focus on what the company needs

Marketing strategy - STP
Segmentation - grouping customers on needs. Profile each segment
Targeting - evaluating the attractiveness of each segment
Positioning - develop your value proposition

How to segment - Who, What, Why
Descriptors - Who are the customers, demographics and psychographics
Behaviour - What customers do, how do they make decisions, responsiveness to marketing mix
Needs - Why do these customers buy

Targeting strategy -
Mass - Target everybody
Multi-target
Niche - Target one segment
Need to consider the potential of that market segment

Positioning - A battle for the customer's mind. Creating an image/ impression of your brand
Significant and meaningful differentiation. Need a good USP (Unique Selling Proposition)
Types of positioning:
* Feature positioning - What's in it?
* Benefit positioning - What's in it for the customers
* Value positioning - Why it's important to the customers

Need a brand positioning statement:
“For $[\text{target market}]$, $[\text{brand}]$ is $[\text{frame of reference, competitive set}]$ that $[\text{key benefits, unique value claim}]$ because $[\text{reasons to believe, evidence}]$.”

Brand equity comes from a high level of awareness and familiarity, and strong favourable and unique brand associations

Evolution of information diffusion
1) Pre digital - Broadcaster (mass media) and editor. All get same content
2) Digital - Everyone can use social networks. Platforms
3) Agent AI - AI recommender + AI creator

What makes the digital era different:
Connectivity - information distribution with little delay
Data - storing and processing data with little cost
Scalability - replicating at zero marginal cost

Customer journey in the digital era: Need-recognition, information search, alternative evaluation, purchase, post-purchase (review)
The post-purchase experience for one customer may influence the alternative evaluation stage for another through reviews
