Transistor really just a switch. It has 3 parameters, ON, OFF and switching
The rise time comes about because of the parasitic resistance and capacitance

History:
Initially Germanium, then used double sided sheet of Germanium - hard to process
Then planar transistor - using photolithography. Then MOSFET then CMOS invented. CMOS useful as only one open at a time, so no rail to rail current, except when switching. Now there are finFETs which etch vertical sheets of silicon for better use of space and to use more of the semiconductor.

The smaller the emitter collector gap, the larger the switching speed or working frequency that can be achieved is.

When the dimensions of a conductor shrink below the electronic mean free path, electrical resistivity starts to change.
Quantum effects are discrete - properties occur in jumps, like resistance
Surfaces have different properties than bulk

We can get an idea for the size of an electron by equating $qV$ to the relativistic rest-mass energy $mc^2$

Double slit experiment - 
For a particle, the total probability mass function is the sum of the 2 PMFs with either slit closed and the other open.
For a wave, it is a diffraction pattern. We see this with electrons too. We note that the intensity is given by $I(x)=I_1+I_2+2\sqrt{I_1I_2}\cos(\theta)$
We detect electrons using phosphorous, which emits a photon each time an electron arrives
We get a diffraction pattern, unless we detect which slit the electron goes through every time, then it disappears.

Photoelectric effect - 
$KE=E-\phi$. Increasing intensity does not change the energy of the electrons, but the number per second.
